Banyan Tree(Ficus benghalensis)

About the Banyan Tree

Ficus benghalensis is an evergreen, monoecious, fast-growing tree found mainly in monsoon and rainforests, that can reach a height of up to 30 meters.
It is resistant to drought and mild frost. It produces propagating aerial roots that grow downward. Once these roots reach the ground, they take root and become supportive woody trunks.

Parts of the Banyan Tree & Their Benefits

Banyan Leaves

Wound Healing:A paste made from banyan leaves can be applied externally to help heal wounds.

Diabetes: Extracts from the banyan tree, including the bark and leaves, have shown promise in managing blood sugar levels and may have antidiabetic effects.

Banyan Bark

Hair Care:Banyan bark decoction can be used to treat scalp itching, promote hair growth, and strengthen hair roots.

Oxidant:It is used in diphtheria and rheumatism, and it is believed to possess a tonic action on mucus membrane.

Scientific Classification

Scientific Name:Ficus benghalensis

Family: Mulberry family (Moraceae)

Banyan Remedies

  • rope and Paper: The bark of the banyan tree is used for making paper and ropes.
  • Eco-Friendly Serving Cups: Banyan leaves are used to create biodegradable serving cups.

Environmental Benefits

Banyan trees offer significant environmental benefits, acting as carbon sinks, providing habitat and food for wildlife, and aiding in soil stabilization and water conservation.
