Mango Tree(Mangifera indica)

About the Mango Tree

Mango trees, scientifically known as Mangifera indica, are large, evergreen fruit trees that can grow up to 100 feet tall and are known for producing the sweet and juicy mango fruit. They are native to South Asia and are now cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions worldwide.

Parts of the Mango Tree & Their Benefits

Mango Leaves

Skin Care: Mango leaf extract can help in managing acne-prone skin, potentially reducing the production of excess sebum, and may also promote wound healing.

Blood Sugar Regulation: Mango leaves are believed to help lower blood sugar levels and may be beneficial for managing diabetes.

Mango Bark

Hair Care:Mango bark decoction can be used to treat scalp itching, promote hair growth, and strengthen hair roots.

Oxidant:It is used in diphtheria and rheumatism, and it is believed to possess a tonic action on mucus membrane.

Mango Seed

Dental: Mango seed powder can be used as a natural tooth powder to promote healthy teeth and gums.

Bone Health:Mango seeds contain essential minerals like calcium, phosphorus, and magnesium, which are important for maintaining strong and healthy bones.

Scientific Classification

Scientific Name: Mangifera indica

Family: Cashews

Mango Remedies

  • Mango Leaf Tea: mango leaves are used to help regulate blood sugar levels. Boil 5-6 tender mango leaves in water, let it sit overnight, and drink the filtered concoction in the morning.
  • Mango Pulp: Mango pulp can be used as a mask to prevent sebum production and remove clogging of pores, potentially helping with acne and blackheads.
  • Mango seed oil:Mango seed oil, mixed with olive or coconut oil, is traditionally used to promote wound healing and reduce swelling.

Environmental Benefits

Mango trees offer significant environmental benefits, including carbon sequestration, soil conservation, and biodiversity promotion
